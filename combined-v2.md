## Technical Setup

I’ve seen merchants spend weeks choosing a theme but skip the fundamentals: proper domains, security, shipping configuration. That’s like decorating a house while the foundation cracks. We’ll lay that foundation together, and I’ll share the shortcuts I wish I’d known.

### Setting Up Your Shopify Store

#### Create Your Store
Sign up for a Shopify plan (the 14‑day trial is enough to validate your idea). Choose a store name—it becomes your temporary `.myshopify.com` URL. Don’t overthink it; you can change it once.

**Why this matters:** Your store name is your first branding decision. Pick something memorable, spellable, and available as a domain. I’ve seen great products fail because the name was impossible to pronounce.

> **Take a Breath**  
> You don’t need the perfect name. You need a *good enough* name so you can start. Perfection is the enemy of progress.

#### Custom Domain
Purchase a domain through Shopify or your registrar (Namecheap, Google Domains, etc.). Connect it in **Online Store > Domains**. Shopify provides free SSL automatically—no extra steps.

**My mistake:** I used a free `.myshopify.com` URL for six months. It looked unprofessional, and I lost trust instantly when I told people my “website address.” Custom domain is non‑negotiable.

#### Store Configuration Basics

- **General settings**: Store address, currency, time zone. Double‑check these—they affect taxes and shipping.
- **Notifications**: Customize order confirmation and shipping emails. Add your brand voice; a generic “Your order has shipped” feels robotic.
- **Payments**: Enable Shopify Payments if available in your region, plus PayPal as a fallback. Don’t offer too many options—just the ones your customers use.
- **Shipping**: Configure zones and rates. I once lost $4,000 in a month because my shipping rules were wrong and I was undercharging. Test with real orders!
- **Taxes**: Let Shopify handle it automatically unless you have special requirements.
- **Checkout**: Keep it simple. Enable account creation optional, disable extra fields. Every extra button hurts conversion.

**Pro tip:** Turn on **Shop Pay**—it’s the fastest checkout and customers love it.

### Essential Shopify Apps

I used to think “more apps = more features.” Wrong. Every app is a brick in your site’s loading time. Audit ruthlessly.

#### Product Reviews & Social Proof

**My favorite:** Loox (photo reviews) or Judge.me (free tier works). Why? Because photos build trust faster than text. I once turned a 1.2% conversion rate into 2.4% by adding photo reviews alone.

**Implementation:** Automate review requests 10 days after delivery. Offer a small incentive (10% off next purchase) but allow organic reviews too.

#### Email & SMS Marketing

Klaviyo is the industry standard, but it’s a beast. If you’re starting, Omnisend is easier. Email/SMS are your highest‑ROI channels—set up these flows immediately:

1. Welcome series (3 emails)
2. Abandoned cart (1‑hour and 24‑hour)
3. Browse abandonment (use sparingly)
4. Post‑purchase cross‑sell
5. Winback (45/60/90 days)

> **Founder’s Confession**  
> I ignored email for a year. My store had 5,000 customers and $0 repeat business. After setting up Klaviyo, 38% of my revenue came from email within six months. Don’t sleep on this.

#### Customer Support

Gorgias or Zendesk if you have a team. For solo founders, Tidio’s free plan works. Integrate with Shopify so you can see orders without switching apps.

#### Shipping & Fulfillment

ShipStation is reliable; Shippo is developer‑friendly. If you have a 3PL, use their dedicated app—it’s usually free.

> **Take a Breath**  
> Shipping is the #1 cause of support tickets. Get it right early and you’ll save hours every week.

#### Compliance & Legal

TermsFeed or Lockdown—generate your privacy policy, terms, and cookie consent. GDPR and CPA aren’t optional if you sell to Europe or California. I got a warning letter once. Not fun.

### Theme Selection & Customization

Start with **Dawn** (free, fast, clean). It’s Shopify’s reference theme for a reason. Customize via **Online Store > Themes > Customize**. For heavy changes, duplicate the theme first.

**Performance tip:** Dawn is lightweight. Avoid “all‑in‑one” themes that promise every feature—they come with hundreds of unused sections that bloat your code.

> **Mastery Mindset**  
> Your theme is not forever. Many merchants obsess over “the perfect theme.” Stop tweaking and start selling. You can always change it later.
